Where Do You Stand?

27 real situations. No right answers. Find out where you actually stand — and who you'd have to argue with.

About 10 minutes  ·  Nothing is stored or sent anywhere

How it works

You'll face concrete situations — not abstract propositions — and pick what you'd actually do. A few questions are forced choices: no middle option. Those are the ones that tell you the most.

At the end you'll see where you lean across six tensions, get questions to argue about, and find out who you'd disagree with.

The six tensions
A note for teachers

This is a discussion tool, not an assessment. It avoids ideology labels and verdicts. Students see a profile across six tensions, reflection questions tailored to their leanings, a "someone who'd disagree" debate prompt, and optional position overlaps (none pinned to the student). Five forced-choice moments and a "hardest question" screen before results surface genuine political judgement rather than comfortable positions. An answer review lets students trace exactly why their profile sits where it does. Nothing is stored or sent anywhere.
