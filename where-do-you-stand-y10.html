Where Do You Stand?

Politics isn't a straight line from left to right. It's a set of tensions — pulls in two directions, where reasonable people land in different places.

You'll face some real situations and decide what you'd do. There are no right answers, and this won't tell you "what you are." It'll show you where you lean, where you'd switch sides, and who you'd have to argue with.

This tool will never label you. It doesn't sort you into a party or an "-ism". The point isn't to find out who you are — it's to help you notice how you think, and to give you something to argue about.

A note for teachers

This is a discussion tool, not an assessment. It deliberately avoids ideology labels and verdicts: students see a profile across six plain-language tensions, a set of reflection questions, a "someone who'd disagree" prompt for debate work, and an optional explainer of positions people hold (none of which is pinned on the student). Each situation is a concrete dilemma rather than an abstract proposition, to measure judgement rather than vocabulary. Use it as a lesson starter, a unit touchstone students revisit, debate preparation, or a take-home. Results can be printed for class discussion. Nothing is stored or sent anywhere.
